Flipkart Sales Dashboard – Analytical Report

The Flipkart dashboard analyzes 100,000 orders and reveals strong business performance with revenue generation of ₹5,067.91M, total profit of ₹1,553.58M, and a profit margin of 24.53%. Operations are heavily weekday-driven, with 72% of all orders and 71.7% of total profit occurring Monday to Friday, indicating stronger business engagement during working days compared to weekends.

Monthly turnover trends reflect relative stability, fluctuating between ₹372M–₹459M, with peak revenue observed in July and a slight drop in October–December. Quarterly revenue analysis shows Q3 as the most profitable period (27% contribution), while Q4 contributes the least (22%), suggesting seasonal demand behavior.

From a market segmentation perspective, the top-performing product categories include Home & Kitchen, Appliances, Fashion, Sports & Fitness, and Footwear, contributing a combined 33.9% of revenue. In brand analysis, Nike, HUL, Philips, Samsung, and Dell lead profitability, each exceeding ₹104M, collectively accounting for 33.9% of total brand-driven profit.

Geographically, the dashboards highlight uneven regional performance. West Bengal is identified as the top revenue state, generating 10.23% of total revenue (₹518.67M). At the city level, Kolkata, Jaipur, Mumbai, Bangalore, and Lucknow drive over 50.40% of profit, emphasizing strong metro-driven demand concentration.

Operational efficiency appears robust with a 95.12% stock clearance rate, supported by 237,547 delivered items and 12,189 units pending. Payment mode analysis identifies Credit Card as the most profitable channel, responsible for 20.06% of transaction revenue (₹1,020.82M).

Business Recommendations

Weekend Campaign Opportunity: Low weekend demand suggests scope for targeted promotional strategies.

Q4 Performance Optimization: Seasonal slowing could be mitigated with festive bundles or discount-based incentives.

Category Expansion: High-performing categories (Home & Kitchen, Appliances) present scaling opportunities with new product lines.

Credit Card Incentivization: Partnerships with banks may increase conversion rates and average transaction values.

Inventory Forecasting: High stock clearance rates support predictive stock planning to reduce leftover units.
